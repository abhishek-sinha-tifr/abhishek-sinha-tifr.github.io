Online Learning: Theory and Algorithms

Piazza signup link


Venue: ESB 106
Slot: E
Office Hours: Friday 6-7 PM (after the class)


The ongoing success story of the learning paradigm has been greatly influenced in part by rigorous advances in the field of online learning. Concepts from online learning theory have also been successfully used in Information Theory and Sequential investments. The aim of this course is to introduce students to the major mathematical techniques and algorithms so that they can effectively read papers and carry out independent research in this field. The course will give a glimpse of both classic and recent results.


Prerequisites: Solid background in undergraduate probability and analysis. Prior exposure to convex optimization, information theory, and machine learning will be helpful but not mandatory.

A. Mathematical preliminaries

  • Brief introduction to Convexity and optimization algorithms
  • Basic Concentration Inequalities, Information-theoretic Inequalities

B. Online Learning with Full-information

  • Prediction with expert advice, Mixture forecaster, Exp-concave losses, universal compression.
  • Dot loss, The Hedge algorithm, regret bounds
  • Online Convex Optimization, Follow-the-regularized leader, Follow-the-perturbed leader
  • Adaptive and Optimistic Algorithms, Switching regret, The Fixed-Share algorithm
  • Connection with Game Theory, Blackwell's approachability
  • PAC-Bayes Generalization bounds from online learning

C. Online Learning with Partial Information

  • Stochastic Multi-armed Bandits, Explore-then-commit, Successive elimination, variants of UCB; Lower bounds
  • Adversarial Bandits, EXP3 class of algorithms
    • Reference: Part V of LS
  • Linear and Contextual Bandits
  • Elements of MDP and Reinforcement Learning


Evaluations

Problem-solving will be our primary vehicle for learning the material. We will have bi-weekly problem sets, a mid-term and a final exam. The final grade will be a weighted average of these three components as detailed below:
  • Problem Sets (50%)
  • Mid-term (20%)
  • Final Exam (30%)
